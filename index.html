Us & The Valley

    In 2016, after our first year of dating, we visited Yosemite as part of a 5 week road trip. We were sharing our first "home" - a rented Ford E-series campervan - and were testing if we were compatible enough to survive together dirty, subsisting on camp food, and sleeping on the side of the road. Turns out we made for pretty good co-pilots.
    In 2021, we returned to the Valley in the midst of a newer adventure: moving to the west coast amid the pandemic. Blah blah...
     Now, in 2024, we invite you to join us in  a return to Yosemite Valley, so we can celebrate blah blah...

Getting there  - Flying
SFO is about 5 hours to Yosemite. SAC is about 3.5 hours. Fresno (FAT) is 2.5 hours. SFO is most likely to have direct flights from the East Coast. But Bay Area traffic can be an unpleasant experience, so SMF may be easier to  escape from.
Getting there -  driving
Yosemite Valley is accessible by car and limited transit options and in April ONLY approachable from the East, not Tioga Pass. YART (the local bus system will only be operating it's service along Highway 140.
Lodging Within Yosemite Valley there are hotels, rustic rooms, and traditional camping options. In West Yosemite community a half hour from the Valley, there are numerous full house Airbnbs.
Getting Around
Once in Yosemite Valley, it is fairly easy to get around by foot, bike, or by the NPS operated shuttle bus.
Food, gas, etc
There are no gas stations in the Valley.
